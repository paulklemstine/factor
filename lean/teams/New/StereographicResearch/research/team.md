# Research Team: Stereographic Projection and Conformal Geometry

## Team Structure

### Principal Investigator
**Conformal Geometry Lead** — Responsible for the overall research direction, theorem selection, and proof strategy. Specializes in the interplay between differential geometry and algebraic structures. Led the discovery of the Fisher-stereographic connection and the formalization of Apollonian dynamics.

### Formal Verification Team
- **Lean 4 Formalization Specialist** — Translates mathematical theorems into precise Lean 4 statements, manages imports and dependencies, handles type coercions between ℕ, ℤ, ℚ, ℝ, ℂ. Responsible for the N-dimensional inverse stereographic projection formalization.
- **Proof Engineer** — Develops proof strategies, decomposes complex theorems into lemmas, and drives the automated proof search. Proved all 35+ theorems including the challenging cross-ratio invariance and Apollonian form preservation.
- **Mathlib Integration Specialist** — Ensures compatibility with the Mathlib library, discovers relevant existing lemmas (e.g., `Real.cosh_sq_sub_sinh_sq`), and maintains code quality standards.

### Applications Team
- **Machine Learning Architect** — Designs the stereographic attention mechanism and Lorentz-equivariant transformer architecture. Developed the chordal distance formula as an attention kernel.
- **Statistical Geometry Researcher** — Discovered the Fisher-stereographic correspondence showing that Bernoulli distributions form a sphere. Developing extensions to exponential families.
- **Quantum Information Theorist** — Formalized the Bloch sphere fidelity formula and is developing stereographic quantum error correction codes.
- **Number Theory Specialist** — Manages the connections to Pythagorean triples, Gaussian integers, and p-adic analysis. Proved the universal ring-theoretic stereographic identity.

### Visualization and Communication Team
- **Scientific Visualization Designer** — Creates SVG diagrams, Python demonstrations, and interactive visualizations of stereographic projection phenomena.
- **Science Writer** — Authors the research paper and Scientific American article, translating formal mathematics into accessible prose.

## Collaboration Model

The team operates in iterative cycles:

1. **Discovery Phase**: Mathematical exploration, conjecture generation, computational experiments
2. **Formalization Phase**: Statement of theorems in Lean 4, skeleton construction with `sorry`
3. **Verification Phase**: Automated and interactive proof search, lemma decomposition
4. **Communication Phase**: Paper writing, visualization, demo construction
