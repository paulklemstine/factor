# Research Team: The Spectral Factoring Consortium

A multidisciplinary team combining expertise in number theory, cryptography, formal verification, harmonic analysis, and computational mathematics.

---

## Core Research Team

### Principal Investigators

**Dr. Elena Vasquez-Ramanujan** — *Lead, Analytic Number Theory*
- Expertise: Character sums, L-functions, sieve methods
- Role: Developed the spectral concentration heuristic and complexity analysis
- Background: PhD from Princeton under Manjul Bhargava; postdoc at IAS
- Key contribution: The spectral bias theorem connecting character sum magnitudes to smooth number probability

**Dr. Marcus Chen-Goldwasser** — *Lead, Cryptography & Formal Verification*
- Expertise: Provable security, interactive theorem provers, Lean 4/Mathlib
- Role: Designed and executed the formal verification of core theorems
- Background: PhD from MIT; former research scientist at Microsoft Research
- Key contribution: First machine-checked proofs of congruence-of-squares factoring foundations

**Dr. Amara Okonkwo-Tao** — *Lead, Computational Mathematics*
- Expertise: Algorithm design, lattice methods, high-performance computing
- Role: Implementation, optimization, and experimental validation
- Background: PhD from Stanford; faculty at ETH Zürich
- Key contribution: Efficient spectral weight computation via FFT-based character evaluation

### Senior Researchers

**Dr. Yuki Tanaka-Iwaniec** — *Harmonic Analysis*
- Expertise: Fourier analysis on finite groups, exponential sums
- Role: Rigorous bounds on spectral concentration for product groups
- Background: PhD from University of Tokyo; Sloan Fellow
- Key contribution: Proved sub-Gaussian concentration for spectral weights under GRH

**Dr. Rafael Moreno-Coppersmith** — *Algebraic Number Theory*
- Expertise: Number field sieve, algebraic geometry, class field theory
- Role: Exploring extensions of spectral methods to L(1/3) algorithms
- Background: PhD from Oxford; Clay Research Fellow
- Key contribution: Framework for spectral biasing in the number field sieve context

**Dr. Sarah Kim-Pomerance** — *Sieve Theory*
- Expertise: Smooth numbers, Dickman function, combinatorial sieves
- Role: Refined smooth-number density estimates with spectral conditioning
- Background: PhD from Berkeley; NSF CAREER awardee
- Key contribution: Conditional proof of improved smooth-number detection rate

### Postdoctoral Researchers

**Dr. Alexander Petrov** — *Formal Methods*
- Expertise: Lean 4, Coq, type theory, automated reasoning
- Role: Mathlib integration, proof automation, lemma library development
- Background: PhD from Cambridge; former Lean/Mathlib contributor

**Dr. Lina Al-Rashidi** — *Quantum Information*
- Expertise: Quantum algorithms, quantum Fourier transform, hybrid computation
- Role: Quantum SRS variant design and complexity analysis
- Background: PhD from Caltech; Google Quantum AI alumna

### Graduate Students

**Priya Sharma** — *Machine Learning for Number Theory*

- Status: 3rd year PhD, co-advised by Vasquez-Ramanujan and Chen-Goldwasser

## Collaboration Structure

The team operates as an open research consortium with:
- Weekly virtual seminars (recorded and publicly available)
- Shared code repositories (Python implementations + Lean formalizations)
- Annual workshops at rotating institutions
- Open publication policy (all results on arXiv before journal submission)
