# Research Team: Ramanujan Frontiers in the Berggren Tree

## Team Structure

### Principal Investigator
**Spectral Graph Theory & Number Theory Lead**
- Expertise: Ramanujan graphs, automorphic forms, expander graphs
- Role: Overall research direction, connections to LPS constructions
- Focus: Proving Ramanujan property for Berggren quotient graphs

### Co-PIs

**Quantum Information Scientist**
- Expertise: Quantum walks, quantum algorithms, spectral graph theory
- Role: Quantum walk analysis on Berggren tree and quotients
- Focus: Designing quantum algorithms that exploit tree structure

**Cryptographer**
- Expertise: Post-quantum cryptography, hash functions, group-based cryptography
- Role: Security analysis of Berggren one-way functions
- Focus: Hardness reductions, implementation, benchmarking

**Formal Verification Specialist**
- Expertise: Lean 4, Mathlib, interactive theorem proving
- Role: Machine verification of all mathematical claims
- Focus: Maintaining the Lean formalization, extending to new results

### Research Associates

**Computational Number Theorist**
- Expertise: Algebraic number theory, modular forms, lattice methods
- Role: Computing eigenvalues of quotient graphs, automorphic connections
- Focus: Numerical verification of Ramanujan property for small primes

**Higher-Dimensional Geometer**
- Expertise: Orthogonal groups, quadratic forms, arithmetic groups
- Role: Generalizing Berggren construction to higher dimensions
- Focus: O(n,1;ℤ) generators and spectral analysis

### Graduate Students

**Student 1: Expander Graph Constructions**
- Project: Implementing and benchmarking Berggren quotient graphs
- Tools: SageMath, NetworkX, MATLAB

**Student 2: Quantum Walk Simulations**
- Project: Simulating quantum walks on Berggren trees
- Tools: Qiskit, Cirq, custom quantum simulators

**Student 3: Cryptographic Protocols**
