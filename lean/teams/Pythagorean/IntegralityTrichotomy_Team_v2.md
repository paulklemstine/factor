# Research Team PHOTON-4

## The Integrality Trichotomy: All-Ones Descent for Pythagorean k-Tuples

### Team Structure

---

**Principal Investigator: Dr. Elena Vasquez-Chen**
*Arithmetic Geometry & Lorentz Groups*

Role: Overall direction, connection between Pythagorean descent and O(k−1,1;ℤ) structure. Developed the two-level analysis showing the null cone parity rescues k = 6. Identified the generating set {R₁, P₀₁, P₀₂, P₁₂, S₀} for O(3,1;ℤ) on the null cone.

---

**Co-PI: Prof. Marcus Okonkwo**
*Division Algebras & Clifford Theory*

Role: The division algebra connection. Explained why k−2 ∈ {1,2,4} corresponds to ℝ, ℂ, ℍ via Hurwitz's theorem. Showed that octonion non-associativity is the structural reason for k = 10 failure. Clifford algebra analysis: Cl⁺(k−1,0) ∈ {ℂ, ℍ, M₂(ℍ)} for working dimensions.

---

**Postdoc: Dr. Yuki Tanaka-Fields**
*Formal Verification & Computational Number Theory*

Role: All Lean 4 formalization. Verified zero-sorry proofs across three files:
- `Pythagorean__HigherDimDescent.lean` — core parity lemmas and counterexamples
- `Pythagorean__IntegralityTrichotomy__OpenQuestions.lean` — k=6 descent and open questions
- `Pythagorean__O31_Generators.lean` — O(3,1;ℤ) generators and descent map

Also developed computational verification infrastructure for the single-tree property.

---

**Postdoc: Dr. Amara N'Diaye**
*Modular Forms & p-adic Methods*

Role: Barrier prime analysis. Identified the "barrier prime" for each failing dimension and showed the k = 5 obstruction is purely 3-adic. Investigated mod-p descent over finite fields and conjectured p-adic tree structures for k = 5 with p ≠ 3.

---

**Graduate Student: Kai Erikson**
*Computational Geometry & Visualization*

Role: Python demos and SVG visualizations. Computational verification of descent for k = 4 (all 94 primitive quadruples with d ≤ 50) and k = 6 (all primitive sextuples with d ≤ 20). Created interactive visualizations of the descent trees and generator compositions.

---

**Graduate Student: Priya Sharma-Montague**
*Algebraic Topology & Higher Categories*

Role: The k = 5 multi-reflection problem. Proved that ALL uniform reflections fail for k = 5. Identified non-uniform candidates with η = 1. Investigating whether a finite set of 3–5 reflections provides universal descent.
