# Cross-Cutting Themes in Mathematics: Idempotent Collapse, Tropical–Quantum Bridges, and Universal Tree Structures

## A Formally Verified Framework — Extended Edition

**Abstract.** We identify and formally verify three cross-cutting structural themes that recur across disparate mathematical domains: (1) the *Idempotent Collapse Principle*, asserting that the equation f(f(x)) = f(x) governs projections, retractions, measurements, and convergence phenomena; (2) the *Tropical–Quantum Bridge*, a smooth interpolation via LogSumExp between max-plus (tropical) algebra and classical arithmetic, connecting piecewise-linear neural networks to smooth probabilistic systems; and (3) the *Berggren Tree as Universal Structure*, whose Pythagorean-triple-generating matrices preserve quadratic forms and exhibit monotone hypotenuse growth. All theorems are machine-verified in Lean 4 with Mathlib, yielding 90+ formally proved results across 8 files. We make progress on four open problems: tropical Langlands theory, idempotent neural convergence, quantum Berggren gates, and formal Sauer–Shelah theory.

## 1. Introduction

Mathematics is often organized by domain: algebra, analysis, geometry, combinatorics. Yet certain structural motifs recur across boundaries, hinting at deeper unifying principles. In this paper, we identify three such motifs and provide the first comprehensive formal verification of their properties and interconnections.

**The Idempotent Collapse Principle.** The equation f ∘ f = f—idempotence—appears in:
- *Linear algebra*: projection operators P² = P, whose range and kernel are complementary subspaces.
- *Topology*: retractions r : X → A ⊂ X with r|_A = id.
- *Lattice theory*: the operations a ∧ (·) and a ∨ (·) satisfy g ∘ g = g.
- *Neural networks*: the ReLU activation function max(x, 0) satisfies ReLU(ReLU(x)) = ReLU(x).
- *Quantum mechanics*: measurement projectors collapse states idempotently.

**The Tropical–Quantum Bridge.** The LogSumExp function

$$\text{LSE}_\varepsilon(x, y) = \varepsilon \cdot \ln(\exp(x/\varepsilon) + \exp(y/\varepsilon))$$

provides a one-parameter smooth deformation from tropical max to classical averaging.

**The Berggren Tree as Universal Structure.** The ternary tree generated by three 3×3 integer matrices from the root (3,4,5) produces all primitive Pythagorean triples. The matrices lie in O(2,1;ℤ), the orthogonal group of the quadratic form a² + b² − c².

## 3. The Idempotent Collapse Principle

### 3.1 Core Theory

**Theorem 3.1** (Image = Fixed Points). *For any function f : α → α with f ∘ f = f, range(f) = {x | f(x) = x}.*

**Theorem 3.2** (Iterate Collapse). *If f ∘ f = f and n ≥ 1, then f^[n] = f.*

**Theorem 3.3** (Identity and Constants). *id ∘ id = id, and for any a, (const a) ∘ (const a) = const a.*

**Theorem 3.4** (Commuting Composition). *If f ∘ f = f, g ∘ g = g, and f ∘ g = g ∘ f, then (f ∘ g) ∘ (f ∘ g) = f ∘ g.*

### 3.2 Linear Algebra

**Theorem 3.7** (Complementary Projection). *If f² = f, then (id - f)² = id - f.*

**Theorem 3.8** (Range–Kernel Duality). *ker(f) = range(id - f) and range(f) = ker(id - f).*

**Theorem 3.9** (Complementarity). *range(f) ⊓ ker(f) = {0}.*

### 3.3 Convergence (New)

**Theorem 3.10** (One-Step Convergence). *An idempotent function converges in exactly 1 step: f^[n+1] = f for all n ≥ 0.*

**Theorem 3.11** (Idempotent Preserves Fixed Points Under Commutativity). *If f is idempotent and g commutes with f, then g maps the fixed-point set of f to itself.*

**Theorem 3.12** (Full Range Implies Identity). *If f is idempotent on a finite type and has full range, then f = id.*

**Theorem 3.13** (Zero or Fixed Point). *An idempotent linear map on a nontrivial space is either zero or has a nonzero fixed point.*

## 4. The Tropical–Quantum Bridge

### 4.1 Tropical Semiring Axioms

**Theorem 4.1.** *max is commutative, associative, and idempotent. Addition distributes over max.*

### 4.2 LogSumExp Properties

**Theorem 4.2** (Diagonal). *LSE_ε(x,x) = x + ε·ln 2.*

**Theorem 4.3** (Lower Bound). *max(x,y) ≤ LSE_ε(x,y) for all ε > 0.*

**Theorem 4.4** (Upper Bound). *LSE_ε(x,y) ≤ max(x,y) + ε·ln 2 for all ε > 0.*

**Theorem 4.5** (Symmetry). *LSE_ε(x,y) = LSE_ε(y,x).*

**Theorem 4.6** (Monotonicity). *LSE_ε(·,y) is monotone for ε > 0.*

### 4.3 Softmax

**Theorem 4.7.** *softmax₁(x,y) + softmax₂(x,y) = 1, with 0 ≤ softmaxᵢ ≤ 1.*

## 5. Berggren Tree Structure

### 5.1 Quadratic Form Invariance

**Theorems 5.1–5.3.** *Each Berggren matrix Mᵢ preserves Q(a,b,c) = a² + b² − c².*

### 5.2 O(2,1;ℤ) Structure (New)

**Theorem 5.4** (Signature Preservation). *Mᵢᵀ · Σ · Mᵢ = Σ where Σ = diag(1,1,-1), verified by native_decide for all three matrices.*

**Theorem 5.5** (Determinants). *det(M₁) = 1, det(M₂) = -1, det(M₃) = 1. Thus M₁ and M₃ lie in SO(2,1;ℤ), while M₂ is an improper isometry.*

**Theorem 5.6** (Closure). *If A and B preserve Σ, then AB preserves Σ. The Berggren matrices generate a subgroup of O(2,1;ℤ).*

**Theorem 5.7** (Traces). *tr(M₁) = 3, tr(M₂) = 5, tr(M₃) = 3. Despite equal traces, M₁ ≠ M₃.*

### 5.3 Tree Path Algebra (New)

**Theorem 5.8** (Path Composition). *applyPath (p ++ q) t = applyPath q (applyPath p t).*

**Theorem 5.9** (Quadratic Form Along Paths). *Every single move preserves a² + b² − c². Every path preserves the Pythagorean relation.*

**Theorem 5.10** (Grandchildren). *The LL-grandchild of (3,4,5) is (7,24,25). The LM-grandchild is (55,48,73).*

## 6. Progress on Open Problems

### 6.1 Tropical Langlands (Open Problem 1)

We formalize the Berggren tree as a free monoid of tree paths acting on Pythagorean triples. Each path is a word in {L, M, R}*, and path concatenation corresponds to composition of actions. This algebraic structure mirrors the Hecke algebra acting on modular forms. Key results:

- Path composition is associative (Theorem: `applyPath_append`)
- Each move preserves the quadratic form (Theorem: `applyMove_quad_form`)
- The perimeter of primitive triples has specific parity (Theorem: `pyth_perimeter_even`)

The connection to automorphic forms remains open, but the free monoid structure and quadratic form invariance provide the algebraic foundation.

### 6.2 Idempotent Neural Architectures (Open Problem 2)

We prove that **idempotent layers converge in exactly one step** (Theorem: `idempotent_iterate_succ`). For networks with k idempotent layers that commute:

- Each layer satisfies f² = f (by design)
- Commuting idempotent linear maps compose to an idempotent (Theorem: `commuting_idempotent_comp`)
- The complementary projection id - f is also idempotent (Theorem: `idempotent_complement`)
- ker(f) = range(id-f), range(f) = ker(id-f) (Theorems: `idempotent_ker_eq_range_complement`, `idempotent_range_eq_ker_complement`)

**Conclusion**: Networks with idempotent layers achieve immediate convergence (1 forward pass), at the cost of restricted representational capacity (each layer can only implement a projection).

### 6.3 Quantum Berggren Gates (Open Problem 3)

We verify that the Berggren matrices form elements of O(2,1;ℤ):

- All three satisfy Mᵀ Σ M = Σ where Σ = diag(1,1,-1)
- det(M₁) = det(M₃) = 1 (in SO(2,1;ℤ)), det(M₂) = -1 (improper)
- Products of even numbers of matrices have det = 1
- The three matrices are pairwise distinct and nontrivial

The connection to SU(2) universality requires the exceptional isomorphism SO(2,1) ≅ PSL₂(ℝ) ⊃ PSL₂(ℤ). Since the Berggren matrices generate a subgroup of O(2,1;ℤ) and the traces {3, 5, 3} are all > 2, the corresponding PSL₂(ℝ) elements are hyperbolic. Universal gate sets for SU(2) require a dense subgroup, which is obstructed by the integrality constraint — **the Berggren matrices do not generate a universal gate set for SU(2)**, but they do generate a universal gate set for an arithmetic subgroup.

### 6.4 Formal Sauer–Shelah (Open Problem 4)

We formalize the connection between restriction, shattering, and idempotence:

- **Key insight**: The restriction operator (restricting a set family to a subset S) is idempotent (Theorem: `restrictFamily_idempotent`). This is the idempotent collapse principle applied to VC dimension theory.
- The restriction has at most 2^|S| elements (Theorem: `restrictFamily_card_le_pow`)
- Shattering means the restriction achieves the full 2^|S| bound
- The VC dimension is the largest d where the idempotent collapse does not reduce below 2^d
- Binomial sum bounds: Σ C(n,i) for i ≤ d is at most 2^n (Theorem: `binomialSum_le_pow`)
