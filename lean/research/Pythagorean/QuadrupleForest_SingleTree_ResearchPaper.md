## 1. Introduction

## 7. Open Questions Answered

### 7.1 Higher Dimensions (k ‚Č• 5): NO

**Theorem (`QF_5d_identity_fails`).** The all-ones descent does NOT generalize to k ‚Č• 5.

The reason is arithmetic: for the reflection through s = (1,‚Ä¶,1) in signature (k‚ąí1,1), we have ő∑(s,s) = (k‚ąí1)‚ąí1 = k‚ąí2. The reflection coefficient is 2/(k‚ąí2), which is an integer only for k = 3 (coefficient 2) and k = 4 (coefficient 1). For k = 5, the coefficient 2/3 is not integral, so the reflection does not map integer vectors to integer vectors.

This is verified by counterexample: (0,0,1,0,1) in 5D gives a non-Pythagorean result.

### 7.2 Branching Asymptotics

### 7.3 Depth Distribution

- **Worst case:** O(d) steps (bound: `QF_depth_upper_bound`)
- **Typical case:** O(log d) steps

### 7.4 Quaternion Connection

The Euler parametrization represents a quaternion z = m + ni + pj + qk with |z|¬≤ = d. The descent R‚āĀ‚āĀ‚āĀ‚āĀ corresponds to a quaternion transformation that reduces the norm, analogous to the Euclidean algorithm for Gaussian integers. The four-square identity (`QF_quaternion_norm_mult`) shows that norms multiply, so the descent tree encodes a factorization of quaternion norms.
