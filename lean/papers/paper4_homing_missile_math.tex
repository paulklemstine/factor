\begin{abstract}
Traditional Inertial Measurement Units (IMUs) suffer from accumulated integration drift over time. This paper proposes a mathematically exact drift-free navigation algorithm for high-velocity guided projectiles by locking coordinate updates to discrete, formally verified Pythagorean and quaternion lattice transitions.
\end{abstract}

\section{The Integration Drift Crisis}
Precision homing missiles rely on gyroscopes and accelerometers that drift due to continuous variable integration errors (Riemann sum decay). We circumvent this by translating physical space into a discrete $\mathbb{Z}[i, j, k]$ quaternionic lattice. 

\section{Lattice-Locked Navigation}
A missile's trajectory vector is constrained to the rational points of a sphere, perfectly modeled by $3D$ Pythagorean quadruples: $a^2 + b^2 + c^2 = d^2$. By applying discrete orthogonal transformations (the $SO(3)$ analogue to the Berggren matrices), the path of the homing missile is guaranteed to snap to the nearest exact rational vertex.
Consequently, measurement noise cannot accumulate: the true physical heading is recovered algorithmically by solving for the closest Diophantine trajectory vector. 

\section{Conclusion}
Mathematical perfection replaces mechanical estimation. Lattice-locked navigation offers absolute precision for atmospheric and orbital intercept systems without relying on external GPS uplinks.
